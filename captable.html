Methodology How the simulator calculates your numbers
01
Post-money & investor ownership
When an investor puts in $1M at a $4M pre-money valuation, post-money = $5M. The investor owns 20%. All existing shareholders are diluted proportionally.
Post-money = Pre-money + Investment
Investor % = Investment ÷ Post-money
Dilution factor = 1 − Investor %
02
Cumulative dilution across rounds
Each round dilutes all existing shareholders. 45% ownership before a 15% Seed round becomes 45% × 85% = 38.25%. This compounds across every round.
Equity after = Equity before × Dilution factor
Total dilution = 1 − ∏(all dilution factors)
03
Exit proceeds distribution
At exit, proceeds are distributed proportionally. 22% ownership at a $50M exit = $11M in proceeds. Simple pro-rata, no liquidation preferences.
Proceeds = Exit valuation × Ownership %
Note: No liquidation preferences applied
04
Investor IRR & CoCa
For each round, IRR and Cash-on-Cash multiple are calculated from ownership at exit vs. investment amount. IRR uses bisection against a 25% VC threshold.
CoCa = Exit proceeds ÷ Investment
IRR: NPV = −Invest + Proceeds÷(1+r)^n = 0
⚠
Important limitations — This simulator provides indicative estimates only. It does not model liquidation preferences, anti-dilution clauses, pro-rata rights, convertible notes, or down rounds. For legally binding cap table modeling, consult a lawyer and use dedicated tools like Carta or Capdesk.
